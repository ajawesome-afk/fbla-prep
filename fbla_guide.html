Elevate Your Future

The National Awards Program (NAP) recognizes and rewards excellence in a broad range of business and career-related areas. It's your time to shine.

Why Compete in FBLA?

  • Career Development: Explore specific career clusters like Finance, Tech, and Marketing.
  • Networking: Meet students and business professionals at District, State, and National levels.
  • Scholarships: Many top winners earn access to exclusive educational funding and internships.
  • Leadership: Develop the soft skills needed to lead in the 21st-century workplace.

Understand Competition Formats

Objective Tests

A 100-question multiple-choice exam taken online. Covers fundamental concepts in specific business areas.

Example: Accounting I, Business Law

Role Play Events

Combines an objective test with a live scenario. You have limited time to solve a problem and present it to a judge.

Example: Entrepreneurship, Management

Presentation

Prepare a speech or project in advance. Use visual aids and technology to convince a panel of judges.

Example: Public Speaking, Digital Video

Production

Skills-based testing involving actual software use (Word, Excel, Coding) to produce specific deliverables.

Example: Computer Applications, Programming

Practice and Simulate

For Objective Tests, use Quizlet and past FBLA practice tests. For Role Plays, practice thinking on your feet with a 10-minute timer. For Presentations, record yourself and watch for filler words (um, ah, like).
